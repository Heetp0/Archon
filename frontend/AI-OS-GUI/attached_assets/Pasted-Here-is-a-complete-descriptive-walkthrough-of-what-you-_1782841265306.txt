Here is a complete, descriptive walkthrough of what you will see and experience when you use the Archon GUI, focusing on how the layout brings the backend's power to life:

### The "Feel" and Startup
When you launch the app, you won't see a standard white Windows application. Instead, it feels like opening a futuristic, stealthy developer console. The entire app is draped in deep "Obsidian" black with slightly elevated panels in a muted charcoal grey. 

When it starts, a borderless loading screen pops up in the center of your monitor. It shows a sleek blue progress bar while it waits for the background FastAPI daemon to boot up. Once the daemon says "I'm ready" over the WebSocket, the loading screen dissolves, and the massive main interface expands into view.

---

### The Permanent Interface (Always Visible)

**The Top System Bar**
Running across the very top of the window is a thin, stealthy header. 
*   On the left, it proudly says **THE CORE** in bold silver letters.
*   Next to it is an **Autopilot** toggle switch that glows emerald green when turned on.
*   On the far right, you get live, real-time telemetry streaming from your PC (e.g., "CPU: 14% | RAM: 8.2GB | LOCAL_HOST: ACTIVE").

**The Left Navigation Rail (The Spine)**
Anchored to the absolute left edge of the screen is a vertical strip of buttons that acts as the spine of the application. It contains icons for your 4 primary modes: Chat, Council, Agents, and Research. Depending on which one you click, the massive workspace on the right instantly hot-swaps to that interface. At the very bottom of this rail sits a Settings gear icon.

**The Context Sidebar (Your Local Brain)**
Sandwiched between the Nav Rail and your main workspace is the Context Sidebar. This is your local file system and vector database (LanceDB) hub. 
*   It has two tabs at the top: **Vault** and **History**. 
*   If you click **Vault**, you see a scrollable, nested list of all your local Markdown notes, code files, and folders that the backend has indexed. This proves the AI has context on your local machine.

---

### The Four "Engines" (The Main Workspace)

#### 1. Normal Chat (The Workhorse)
If you click the Chat icon, the main workspace turns into a clean, modern messaging interface. 
*   At the very top, there is a dropdown menu. Because the GUI talks to your backend, this dropdown *dynamically* reads your API keys and only shows you models you actually have access to (like GPT-4o, Claude 3.5, or local Ollama models).
*   The center is a massive scrolling chat history. Your messages appear on the right in subtle grey bubbles, and the AI’s responses stream in from the left.
*   At the bottom is the input bar. What makes this special is the **`+` (Attachment) button**. When you click it, a file picker opens. If you select a `.py` script or a `.pdf`, a small "chip" appears above your text box showing the file is attached. When you hit send, the backend silently rips the text out of that file and injects it into the LLM's brain along with your message!

#### 2. Council Debate (The War Room)
If you click the Council icon, the interface transforms into a multi-agent "War Room."
*   Instead of one model selector, you now see **four** dropdowns at the top. You can assign different AIs to different roles (e.g., Claude as the Proposer, GPT-4 as the Critic, Gemini as the Expert).
*   The screen splits vertically. The left column is the **Proposer's podium**, where the initial idea or code structure is pitched. 
*   The right side is a wider **Debate Canvas**. Once the Proposer finishes, you will literally watch the Critic and the Domain Expert start streaming their counter-arguments and refinements into this canvas at the same time, arguing back and forth. 

#### 3. System Agents Console (The Hacker Terminal)
Press `Ctrl+T` (or click the Agents icon), and you are taken to a developer operations terminal.
*   The top half features a clean dashboard card called the "Active Task Checklist." As the backend AI decides to execute a multi-step plan, it dynamically updates this checklist so you can watch its thought process unfold step-by-step.
*   The bottom half is a massive, true-black terminal window with matrix-green text. 
*   If you type a command into the input bar at the bottom (like "Analyze my Python scripts for bugs"), the terminal will start violently scrolling with green text as the backend agent navigates your file system, reads code, and executes bash commands on your behalf. 
*   *(Note: If the AI tries to run a dangerous command, a giant Warning Modal will suddenly pop up over the whole screen, forcing you to click "Approve" or "Deny" before the backend proceeds.)*

#### 4. Deep Research (The Web Scraper)
Clicking the Research icon gives you an interface built for massive data gathering.
*   You type a broad query into a large search bar at the top (e.g., "Find the latest research on solid-state batteries").
*   The center of the screen is reserved for a visual graph canvas (currently a placeholder for a mind-map of sources).
*   At the bottom is a scrolling "Live Log Feed." While you sit back, you watch the backend Tavily/Playwright engines stream their status updates: *"Googling solid state batteries..."* -> *"Found 14 links..."* -> *"Scraping MIT article..."* -> *"Summarizing..."*

### The Settings Modal
Whenever you click the gear icon in the bottom left, a smooth window overlays the entire app. It has tabs on the side. This is where you paste your API keys (which turn into `***` for security). The moment you click **Save**, the GUI sends a WebSocket burst to the FastAPI daemon, which instantly updates its `.env` file—meaning you never have to restart the app to change your AI models!
